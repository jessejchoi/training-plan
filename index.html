01

Pre-Run Activation

~8–10 min · Dynamic only

Dynamic only — no static holds before running. Goal is to wake up the posterior chain, open the hips, and prime neuromuscular patterns.

01Leg Swings — Sagittal 10 reps / side Watch
Stand on one leg, hold a wall lightly. Swing the free leg forward and back through full range — ballistic, momentum-driven. Start small, build to full ROM over the first few reps.
↗ Hip flexor opening · Hamstring dynamic stretch
02Leg Swings — Frontal 10 reps / side Watch
Same stance, swing leg side to side across the body. Cross midline on the inward swing. Targets hip abductors/adductors and deep rotators needed for lateral hip stability during stance phase.
↗ Hip rotator mobility · Adductor opening
03Hip Circles (Hurdle Walk) 8 reps / side Watch
Walking forward, draw a large circle with each knee — up, out, back behind you like stepping over a hurdle. Alternate legs each step. Hits the full hip circumduction range.
↗ Full hip circumduction · Glute med activation
04Walking Lunge + Rotation 8 reps / side Watch
Step into a lunge, drop the back knee. At the bottom, rotate torso toward front leg — reach inside arm toward sky. Hold 1–2 sec, step through.
↗ Hip flexor · Thoracic rotation · Ankle dorsiflexion
05Ankle Circles + Toe/Heel Walk 10 circles + 15m each Watch
Draw full circles with each ankle, both directions. Then walk 15m on toes, 15m on heels. Primes full ankle range and calf complex before any foot-strike loading.
↗ Ankle ROM · Calf/shin activation
06High Knees (Slow, Exaggerated) 20m Watch
Not a conditioning drill — slow and deliberate. Drive one knee to hip height, dorsiflexed foot, step down controlled. Reinforces hip flexion pattern needed at interval pace.
↗ Hip flexion ROM · Stride pattern priming
07Glute Bridge Hold 2 × 10 sec hold Watch
Lie on back, feet flat, drive hips up and hold. Squeeze glutes hard — don't rely on lower back. Two 10-second holds wakes up the posterior chain without fatiguing it.
↗ Glute activation · Hip extension prep
02

Post-Run Flexibility

~12–15 min · Static + PNF

Primary flexibility window. Tissues are warm. Hold everything long — 45–60 seconds minimum. Breathe into each stretch.

Jakarta heat note: You're well warmed up after any outdoor run. Don't rush this — use the cooldown window before the AC hits you. Tissues cool faster than you think indoors.
01Standing Quad Stretch 60 sec / side Watch
Pull heel to glute. Keep pelvis neutral — don't let the stretching hip drift forward. For deeper stretch, gently extend the hip backward.
↗ Rectus femoris length · Hip flexor secondary
02Low Lunge / Couch Stretch 60 sec / side Watch
Kneeling lunge, back knee on ground, front foot forward. Tuck pelvis under slightly and lean forward. Couch stretch variant (back foot elevated on wall) dramatically increases intensity.
↗ Iliopsoas · Hip flexor complex · Key for toe-off mechanics
03Seated Hamstring Stretch 60 sec / side Watch
One leg extended, other bent. Hinge at the hip, spine long, chest toward shin. The stretch comes from hip flexion, not spinal rounding.
↗ Hamstring length · Prevents posterior pelvic tilt at speed
04Figure-4 / Piriformis Stretch 90 sec / side Watch
Lie on back. Cross one ankle over opposite knee. Pull uncrossed leg toward you, or press the crossed knee away. Given your piriformis history, give this one extra time.
↗ Piriformis · Deep external rotators · Glute med
05Calf Stretch — Gastrocnemius 60 sec / side Watch
Against a wall, back leg straight, heel pressed flat. Lean forward. Straight knee targets gastrocnemius — the main calf muscle that crosses the knee.
↗ Gastrocnemius length · Achilles load reduction
06Calf Stretch — Soleus 60 sec / side Watch
Same position, bend the back knee slightly. Shifts stretch into soleus and distal Achilles. The soleus works hardest at slower speeds and in late stance phase.
↗ Soleus · Achilles complex · Ankle dorsiflexion
07Adductor / Groin Stretch 60 sec / side Watch
Wide-legged seated or standing. Shift weight to one side, bending that knee while other stays straight. Or: seated butterfly, press knees toward ground with elbows.
↗ Adductor length · Pelvic stability in stance
08Thoracic Rotation Stretch 8 reps / side Watch
Seated, one hand behind head, other on knee. Rotate torso, driving elbow toward ceiling. Hold 2 sec at end range. Thoracic mobility directly enables arm swing and counter-rotation.
↗ Thoracic rotation · Running economy via arm swing
03

Daily Standalone Session

~15–20 min · Morning or evening

Do this on its own on easy days. Accumulates the mobility gains that single post-run windows can't fully address.

0190/90 Hip Stretch 90 sec / side Watch
Sit on the ground with both hips at 90° — one leg in front (external rotation), one behind (internal rotation). Sit tall, pelvis level. Lean forward over the front shin for deeper stretch. Highest-ROI hip mobility exercise for runners.
↗ External AND internal hip rotation · Piriformis · Glute med
02World's Greatest Stretch 5 reps / side (slow) Watch
From lunge, place same-side hand inside front foot. Rotate inside arm toward sky. Return, do elbow-to-ground. One rep hits: hip flexors, adductors, thoracic rotation, and hamstrings.
↗ Multi-joint mobility · Best all-in-one runner's movement
03Deep Squat Hold 2 × 60 sec Watch
Feet shoulder-width, toes slightly out. Squat as deep as possible, heels on ground. Hold a door frame for support if needed. Targets ankle dorsiflexion, hip flexion depth, and posterior chain length.
↗ Ankle dorsiflexion · Hip flexion depth · Posterior chain
04Cat-Cow + Thread the Needle 10 reps each Watch
On all fours: cat-cow 10 reps. Then thread the needle — slide one arm underneath, rotating thoracic spine, press shoulder toward floor. Hold 2 sec. Switch.
↗ Thoracic + lumbar mobility · Anti-kyphosis
05Supine Hip Flexor (Contract-Relax) 3 × 15 sec / side Watch
Lie at the edge of a bed. Drop one leg off, gravity stretches the hip flexor. Hold 10 sec, contract against gravity for 5 sec, relax 15 sec. PNF technique doubles the stretch gain vs. passive alone.
↗ Iliopsoas · Key for stride extension
06Cossack Squat 8 reps / side Watch
Wide stance, feet forward. Shift weight to one side, bending that knee deeply while the other stays straight. Sit into the bent side, heel flat. Return through center.
↗ Adductor · Hip internal rotation · Ankle mobility
04

Hip Focus Protocol

~10 min · 2–3× per week

Given your piriformis history and hip extension deficit in your stride, these get priority. Do this after easy runs or as part of the standalone session. Not pre-run.

Stride note: Your form issue — stride window too far forward, insufficient hip extension at toe-off — is almost certainly related to hip flexor tightness restricting hip extension range. These exercises directly address it.
01Pigeon Pose (Extended) 2 min / side Watch
From downward dog, bring one shin forward. Square hips to ground. Walk hands forward, torso comes down. Two full minutes per side. Breathe.
↗ Piriformis · Glute med · Deep external hip rotators
02Hip Internal Rotation Stretch 90 sec / side Watch
Lie on back, knees bent. Let both knees fall to one side (windshield wiper). Hold where you feel the outer hip of the top leg stretching. Most runners are biased toward external rotation — this balances it.
↗ Hip internal rotation · Piriformis balance · Glute med
03Prone Hip Extension Stretch 60 sec / side Watch
Lie face down. Bend one knee, let that leg rotate outward so the knee drops toward ground. Gently press hip into floor. Stretches hip flexors in their lengthened end range — the exact position restricted in your toe-off.
↗ Hip flexor end-range · Directly addresses stride extension deficit
04Glute Med Side-Lying Stretch 90 sec / side Watch
Lie on side, legs stacked. Cross top knee over and place that foot flat on floor in front. Rotate top hip forward until you feel a stretch in the outer hip/glute. Lengthens glute med and TFL.
↗ Glute med · TFL · Lateral hip stability
05Hip Flexor PAILs / RAILs 2 min / side (full cycle) Watch
Low lunge: hold 1 minute passively. Press front foot into floor (~20% effort) for 10 seconds. Then push back knee into floor for 10 seconds. Release and relax 30 seconds. Most efficient method for gaining permanent hip flexor length.
↗ Progressive hip flexor gain · Best ROI for stride extension
05

Weekly Integration

How it fits your training week

Mobility works through accumulated volume over weeks. Make pre- and post-run routine automatic on every run day — add the standalone session 2–3× per week for cumulative gains.

Weekly Schedule

Tuesday — Intervals
Pre-run activation → Run → Post-run flexibility. Skip standalone — recovery is priority.
Wednesday — Easy
Pre-run → Run → Post-run + Standalone + Hip protocol. Best full mobility day.
Thursday — Threshold
Pre-run → Run → Post-run flexibility. Same as Tuesday.
Friday — Easy
Pre-run → Run → Post-run. Add hip focus if feeling tight.
Saturday — Easy / Off
Standalone + hip protocol (even without a run). Low-load, high-benefit.
Sunday — Long Run
Pre-run → Run → Extended post-run (15–20 min). Longest hold durations.
Monday — Rest
Optional 10-min standalone if stiff. Otherwise full rest.
Progression: Weeks 1–3, build the habit. From week 4, add PNF contract-relax to your tightest areas. Measurable ROM gains typically appear after 6–8 consistent weeks.
01

Training Principles

Runner-specific strength

Strength training for distance runners isn't bodybuilding. The goal is force production, single-leg stability, and injury resilience — everything below directly supports your running mechanics.

Structure: Two sessions per week. Workout A (Tuesday, after intervals) is heavy bilateral compound work. Workout B (Friday, after easy run) is single-leg dominant at lower loads. Hard days cluster together to protect recovery days.
Piriformis caution: Single-leg stabilizer-dependent exercises (RDLs, split squats) need extra care when performed after hard runs. If your hip is fatigued or achy, reduce load or skip the unilateral work that session.
Loading intent: Workout A — heavy, 3–4 sets, ~4–6 reps (strength zone). Workout B — moderate, 3 sets, ~8–10 reps (strength-endurance, single-leg control priority). Deload every 4th week: reduce volume by ~40%.
02

Workout A — Lower Compound

Tuesday · After intervals · ~35 min

Heavy bilateral work while the nervous system is primed. Focus on posterior chain dominance — glutes and hamstrings are the engine of your running stride.

Workout A · Tuesday
01Barbell Back Squat 4 × 4–6 reps Watch
Primary bilateral lower-body compound. Aim for slightly above parallel depth minimum. Drive through the full foot. Hard brace before each rep — intra-abdominal pressure is your spinal protection.
4 sets4–6 reps2–3 min rest
↗ Quad · Glute max · Lower-body force production
02Romanian Deadlift (Bilateral) 4 × 5–6 reps Watch
Cornerstone posterior chain exercise for runners. Hinge at the hip, maintain neutral spine, feel the hamstring stretch before driving back up. Bar stays close to the body throughout.
4 sets5–6 reps2–3 min rest
↗ Hamstring · Glute max · Spinal erectors
03Leg Press 3 × 6–8 reps Watch
Sub: goblet squat with 3-second eccentric if no leg press. Higher foot placement increases glute/hamstring involvement. Don't lock out the knees at the top.
3 sets6–8 reps90 sec rest
↗ Quad strength · Knee extension power · Running economy
04Nordic Hamstring Curl 3 × 4–6 reps Watch
Kneel with feet anchored. Lower toward ground by slowly extending at the knee, controlling the fall. Push up with hands to return. Start with 3–4 reps if new to these — they're deceptively hard.
3 sets4–6 reps2 min rest
↗ Hamstring eccentric strength · Injury resilience
05Calf Raises — Heavy (Straight Leg) 4 × 8 reps + 3sec eccentric Watch
Standing, weighted. Slow 3-second eccentric, pause at bottom, full range concentric. Calf strength is massively undertrained in distance runners and directly impacts running economy. Load this heavier than feels natural.
4 sets8 reps90 sec rest
↗ Gastrocnemius strength · Achilles resilience · Push-off power
06Plank Variations 3 × 30–45 sec each Watch
Rotate through: forearm plank, side plank (each side), RKC plank. Side plank targets obliques and glute med — the lateral stability chain that prevents trunk sway while running.
3 × each variation30–45 sec hold45 sec rest
↗ Anti-rotation core · Lateral stability · Running posture
03

Workout B — Single-Leg & Control

Friday · After easy run · ~30 min

Single-leg dominant, moderate loads. Goal is hip stability, glute med strength, and unilateral force production — the mechanics that actually transfer to running. Pelvis control matters more than load here.

Workout B · Friday
01Bulgarian Split Squat 3 × 8–10 reps / side Watch
Rear foot elevated on bench. Dumbbells in hand. Lower until front thigh is parallel to ground — front shin roughly vertical. The single-leg demand makes this the closest strength exercise to running mechanics. Front knee tracks over foot, not caving inward.
3 sets / side8–10 reps90 sec rest
↗ Unilateral quad · Glute max · Hip stability · Running transfer
02Single-Leg RDL (Supported) 3 × 8–10 reps / side Watch
Light fingertip contact on wall — correct form, not a crutch. Goal is hamstring and glute loading with a level pelvis. Hinge at the hip, spine neutral. Dumbbell in opposite hand. Keep the pelvis square — don't let the raised hip rotate open.
3 sets / side8–10 reps90 sec rest
↗ Unilateral hamstring/glute · Pelvic control · Hip hinge pattern
03Step-Up (Lateral + Forward) 3 × 10 reps / side Watch
Box ~20–30cm high. Do 5 lateral step-ups then 5 forward step-ups per side. The lateral variant directly loads the glute medius — responsible for preventing hip drop during running. Drive through the heel of the elevated foot. No momentum.
3 sets / side5 lateral + 5 forward60 sec rest
↗ Glute med · Hip abductor · Prevents hip drop in stance
04Single-Leg Calf Raise (Eccentric) 3 × 10–12 reps / side Watch
Stand on one foot on edge of a step, heel hanging off. Rise with both feet, shift to one, lower slowly over 3–4 seconds. Gold standard for Achilles tendon health and calf resilience.
3 sets / side10–12 reps90 sec rest
↗ Soleus · Achilles loading · Unilateral push-off power
05Banded Clamshell + Hip Abduction 3 × 15 reps / side Watch
Resistance band around thighs. Side-lying: 10 clamshells (rotate knee open, feet together) then 5 straight leg raises. Targets the glute medius in isolation — maintaining hip height during every stride's single-leg stance phase.
3 sets / side10 clams + 5 abductions60 sec rest

Weekly Integration

Fitting strength into your run week

Strength always comes after running on the same day — never before. Hard days cluster together so that Wednesday, Saturday, Sunday, and Monday can be recovery-oriented.

Weekly Structure

Tuesday
Interval run → Workout A. ~35 min after the run.
Wednesday
Easy run only. No strength. Full mobility session instead.
Thursday
Threshold run only. No strength — let the threshold adaptation land.
Friday
Easy run → Workout B. Lighter loads; watch the hip on tired legs.
Saturday
Easy run or off. Mobility only. No strength.
Sunday
Long run only. No strength.
Monday
Full rest. Light mobility only if needed.
Deload protocol: Every 4th week, reduce sets by 1 and weight by ~30–40%. Do not skip deload weeks in the 8 weeks before a race.
Race taper: Final 2 weeks before target race — drop to 1 session/week (Workout B only, reduced). Race week — skip strength entirely from Wednesday onward.
